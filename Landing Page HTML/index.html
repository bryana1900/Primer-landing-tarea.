Sequence Adverbs

The adverbs of order first, next, then,after that and finally are used to describe the order in which two or more actions happen.

We use a comma after first, next, then,after that when they introduce the sentence that describes the action.

• First: for an action that happens before any others. We can also use first at the end of the sentence.

• I have a lot of work to do. First, I will make a cup of tea."

next and then can both be used for an action that happens after another action but we usually use:

• Next: for an action that happens immediately after another action, or as soon as possible. We can also use "next" at the end of the sentence. • "I went to Bolivia on holiday. "Next", I want to go to Brazil."

Then: for an action that happens after another action if it’s not immediate. • "Sarah is doing her degree. Then, she wants to be a lawyer."

Finally: for the action that happens at the end, after any others, or after a long time. • "I worked all evening. Finally, I went to bed at 11pm."

Pan cake

Ingredients

• 1½ cups all-purpose flour

• 3½ teaspoons baking poder

• ¼ teaspoon salt, or more to taste

• 1 tablespoon white sugar

• 1¼ cups milk

• 1 egg

• 3 tablespoons butter, melted

First, In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, salt and sugar. Then, make a well in the center and pour in the milk, egg and melted butter; mix until smooth. Next, heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium-high heat. Pour or scoop the batter onto the griddle, using approximately 1/4 cup for each pancake. After that brown on both sides. Finally, serve hot with any toppings of your choice
